\section{Experimental Setup}

\paragraph{Current experimental scope.}
All results available at the time of writing use
\texttt{allenai/OLMoE-1B-7B-0924-Instruct} (16 MoE layers, 64 experts,
top-$k=8$).  The available corpora are GSM8K (1,319 generated traces),
PRM800K (10,833 given-solution traces), and ProcessBench (400 given-solution
traces).  Router tensors are available for every trace.  Geometry--routing
results are available for GSM8K and ProcessBench; expert-event results are
also available for these two datasets.  Unless stated otherwise, event
windows contain five tokens on each side of the event.

\paragraph{Implemented analysis pipeline.}
The repository implements trace generation or ingestion (Experiment 1),
token-level router and optional hidden-state extraction (Experiment 2),
event-centered routing with matched within-trace controls, geometry--routing
correlation (Experiment 3), prospective prefix probes (Experiment 4), and
expert phase/co-activation/transition analysis (Experiment 5).  Offline tools
also support high-precision relabelling, rebuilding multi-dataset taxonomy
summaries, full-trace layerwise linear probes over router, hidden, or combined
features, and a trace-length/structure baseline.  The full pipeline runs
Experiments 1--5 and extracts hidden states by default; the latter and
Experiment 4 can be disabled together when storage is constrained.

\paragraph{Prospective probe design and status.}
Experiment 4 pools only the first 25\%, 50\%, or 75\% of each trace and trains
separate trace-grouped logistic probes from structure-only, router-only,
hidden-only, and combined hidden/router features.  It evaluates final
incorrectness and whether a gold first error remains beyond the observed
prefix, excluding traces whose error has already occurred for the latter
target.  Outputs include out-of-fold AUROC, balanced accuracy, F1, and
trace-bootstrap confidence intervals.  This pipeline is implemented and
tested, but its GPU-dependent hidden-state extraction and empirical probe run
are not yet complete; consequently no Experiment 4 result is reported below.

\paragraph{Important provenance note.}
The event-label heuristic was made substantially more conservative after the
first experimental run.  Taxonomy, event-routing, and expert-event outputs
have now been regenerated from the corrected labels.  The only stale quantity
still displayed is the backtracking-conditioned geometry correlation, because
hidden states were not persisted and must be extracted again on a GPU.

\subsection{Hidden-state geometry and routing}

For each layer, we compute the pairwise cosine-similarity matrix of sampled
token hidden states and the cosine-similarity matrix of their softmax router
distributions.  Their upper triangles are correlated; the overall
significance test is a 1,000-permutation Mantel test.  This is a geometry--
routing correlation analysis, not t-SNE and not a linear-separability probe.
The exposed hidden state is the input to the transformer layer and is the
closest available proxy for, but not exactly, the post-attention state seen by
that layer's router.

Figure~\ref{fig:geometry} and Table~\ref{tab:geometry-summary} show a strong,
replicated layerwise relationship.  Mean overall correlation is 0.562 on
GSM8K and 0.561 on ProcessBench.  It rises sharply from approximately 0.24 in
layer 0 to approximately 0.68 in layers 3--5, then remains around 0.52--0.61
in later layers.  Every layer reaches the permutation-resolution floor
$p=1/1001\approx0.000999$.

\subsection{Trace-grouped linear routing probes}

We train a separate linear logistic probe at each layer using one pooled
router-feature vector per trace.  The 197 features comprise router-probability
means and standard deviations, top-1 expert frequencies, entropy and margin
statistics, and top-1 switch rate.  Five-fold stratified cross-validation
produces out-of-fold predictions; 95\% intervals bootstrap traces.  Since each
trace contributes one sample, token leakage across folds is impossible.  The
probe pools the \emph{complete} trace, so this is a decoding/separability test,
not online prediction of a future error.

PRM800K, and 3/3 on ProcessBench.

\section{Analysis}

\subsection{Conclusions supported so far}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Hidden-state geometry strongly predicts router-distribution geometry at
every layer on both tested datasets.  The effect is strongest in early-middle
layers and replicates closely across GSM8K and ProcessBench.
\item Correct and failed traces have nearly identical geometry--routing
correlations on average.  The direction of the small difference is not stable
across datasets.
\item At 207 gold ProcessBench first errors, entropy, switching, overlap, and
margin do not exhibit a statistically resolved discontinuity relative to a
matched position in the same trace.  Simple layer-averaged routing metrics are
therefore not sufficient first-error markers in the current analysis.
\item PRM800K gives a different first-error signature over 4,315 traces:
switching decreases, overlap increases, and margin increases relative to the
matched control.  This is a small stabilization effect rather than the
hypothesized disruption and does not replicate on ProcessBench.
\item High-precision explicit backtracking/self-correction is much rarer than
the original heuristic suggested.  The relabelling invalidates the larger old
event samples and makes new data collection or broader event annotation
necessary.
\item Full-trace router features linearly decode correctness above a simple
length/structure baseline on all three datasets.  They also decode PRM800K
contradiction (best-layer AUROC 0.908), although rare-class F1 remains low.
These probes establish decodability, not advance prediction or causality.
\item Expert usage varies descriptively across phases and top-1 routes persist
about 20\% of the time, but explicit event samples remain too small for robust
event-specific expert claims.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{What has not been established}

The present experiments do not identify ``math experts'' or ``failure
experts'', do not show that routing changes cause reasoning errors, and do not
show that backtracking forms a linearly separable hidden-state class.  No
t-SNE/UMAP visualization, hidden-state probe, prospective failure detector,
or routing intervention has yet been run.  The completed router probes see the
entire trace; they cannot establish that an error was predictable before it
occurred.  Correlation and linear decodability should not be read as causal
evidence.

\section{Discussion}

The strongest current story is geometry-first: routing follows the evolving
representation space, with a consistent layerwise signature across two very
different trace sources.  The anticipated story of a universal sharp routing
disruption at errors is not supported: ProcessBench is null, while PRM800K
shows a small stabilization.  At the same time, layerwise full-trace probes
decode outcomes much better than local aggregate discontinuities do.  Future
prediction models should therefore retain layerwise and temporal structure
and test only prefixes ending before the annotated error.

The event-label audit also changes the experimental priority.  Explicit
lexical markers provide high precision but extremely low recall.  Robust
backtracking conclusions will require either deliberately elicited
self-checking traces, manual/LLM-assisted span annotation with validation, or
a benchmark with revision spans.  Until then, the gold ProcessBench error
analysis and outcome-based correct/failed comparisons are the safest results.

\section{Conclusion}

So far, OLMoE routing is strongly aligned with hidden-state geometry, but
aggregate routing metrics neither distinguish correct from failed geometry in
a dataset-invariant way nor provide a consistent first-error discontinuity
across datasets.  Full-trace router probes nevertheless decode correctness and
PRM800K contradiction beyond simple structural baselines.  Event-specific
expert results remain underpowered because a label audit removed most
backtracking and self-correction cases.  The next phase is GPU extraction of
hidden states, prefix-only hidden/router probes, better event supervision, and
only then causal routing intervention.
